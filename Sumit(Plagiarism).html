PLAGIARISM

The word Plagiarism is derived from the latin word 'Plagiare',which means to kidnap or abduct.In other english,it refers to the act of using another person's literary work,whether oral or written,and passing it as your own,without giving credit to the original creator.It is considerd as as intellectual theft and is punishable offence. Plagirasim may be unintentinal or intentional .It is a severe violation of academic rights and an unethical practice as it involve stealing other's ideas and using their hardwork to one's own gain and fame.

Types of Plagirasim:-

  • 1. Direct plagirasim:
Done with the express intent of stealing ,it involve passing another's work as one's own intentially.An example of direct plagirasim is a students stealing another student's notes to pass in exam.
  • 2. Paraphasing:-
It involves changing of few words and altering the sentence structure a little to escape from being caught.It is serious as offense as direct plagirasim.

  • 3. Patchwork plagirasim:-
It is similar to paraphasing,except that here ideas or content are stolen from sources and the gelled together.It is most popular in academic world.

  • 4. Mosaic plagirasim:-

In this type,a person uses another's content in his/her work ,without using quotation mark.It also involves replacing words with thier synonyms without using much alteration to the structure or general idea.
  • 5. Accidental plagirasim:-

Sometimes we accidentally cite the source of the content taken from the internet or other book,misquotes their source,or unintentionally paraphase the origina content.This may be unplanned.
  • 6. Self-plagirasim:-
In this form of plagirasim,a writer takes the content from his previous work without citing that the work has also been used previously.A common cases is of submitting the same paper for two assignments for two courses.

Reported Cases of Plagiarism

  • 1. James A MacKay

This Scottish historian was forced to remove several of his works from sale during the course of the 1980s for having plagiarised a number of previous works on the same subject matter: Alexander Graham Bell, Mary Queen of Scots, William Wallace and Andrew Carnegie. Though he was never sued for these plagiarisms (because he withdrew the offending titles) his reputation was destroyed.

  • 2. Marks Chabedi

Academic plagiarism doesn’t come more shocking than when a University Professor is found to have plagiarised his entire PhD thesis from another source. That is exactly what happened with this South African man when he copied the thesis of a professor of the same field working in Florida (a woman by the name of Kimberly Lanegran). He lost his job, had his PhD revoked and the journal in which it appeared had to publish a retraction shortly afterward.

Punishment for Plagiarism In India

  1. The violation of an author’s right to be credited and copyright infringement are both civil wrongs and criminal charges can also be filed against the violator.

  2. In case of a civil suit, the remedies that can be awarded are : injunctions to restrain further infringement, damages, the delivery of accounts of profit and both infringing copies of the work used to make them. Certain administrative actions may also be taken.

  3. A convicted infringer is liable to be imprisoned between six months and three years, and to be fined between fifty thousand and two lakh rupees under section 63 of the Act. The punishment is enhanced for subsequent convictions.

  4. Sometimes College and University rules for student conduct takes Plagiarism as an academic offense and not a legal offense. But this is not the actual case. Plagiarism is not only an academic offence but also a legal one.
